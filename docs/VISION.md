# Trip Commons: Product Vision

## Mission

Trip Commons helps everyone on a trip share the understanding of it.

It gives planners a dependable place to organize the many details they would otherwise have to carry in their heads, while giving every traveler a clear, current view of the plan without requiring them to become a planner themselves.

## The problem

Trips are collective experiences, but planning them is often invisible and unevenly distributed.

One or two people gather ideas, compare options, make reservations, remember constraints, track confirmations, and hold the evolving shape of the trip in their heads. Everyone else sees fragments through messages, forwarded emails, calendar events, spreadsheets, and occasional conversations. The planner becomes both the system of record and the person everyone asks for an update.

This creates two related burdens:

- Planners carry too much information and coordination responsibility.
- Other travelers struggle to understand what has been decided, what is changing, and whether anything is expected of them.

The problem is not simply that trip information is scattered. It is that the group lacks a shared understanding of the trip.

## The vision

Trip Commons becomes the living, shared home for a trip—from the first idea through the journey itself.

It brings together the trip's possibilities, decisions, itinerary, reservations, responsibilities, documents, and updates. It gives the group one current source of truth while respecting that different travelers want and need different levels of involvement.

A planner can work with depth: collecting possibilities, comparing choices, arranging days, managing logistics, and noticing what remains unresolved. Another traveler can open the same trip and immediately understand its shape, see what changed, know what is happening next, and act on the few things that need their attention.

Everyone shares the trip. Not everyone has to manage it.

## Who it serves

Trip Commons is for the whole travel party, not only the primary planner.

People may naturally take on different roles from trip to trip:

- A lead planner shapes and coordinates most of the trip.
- Co-planners take ownership of particular destinations, days, or decisions.
- Contributors add ideas, preferences, information, or occasional help.
- Travelers primarily need awareness, confidence, and timely direction.

These roles should describe participation, not create rigid hierarchies. Every traveler should be able to understand the shared plan, and participation should be easy to increase or reduce as the trip evolves.

## The product layers

### 1. Shared overview

The simplest, clearest expression of the trip: where the group is going, when, with whom, the shape of the journey, its current planning state, and the most important next actions or decisions.

### 2. Planning space

A flexible place to collect ideas, links, places, options, notes, preferences, and open questions before they become commitments. Possibilities remain distinct from decisions.

### 3. Itinerary

A living day-by-day plan that can hold both fixed events and flexible intentions. It should communicate structure without making travel feel over-scheduled.

### 4. Logistics

The dependable home for transportation, lodging, reservations, confirmation details, documents, check-in information, and other practical facts the group may need.

### 5. Tasks and decisions

A lightweight way to make unresolved work visible: what needs to happen, what requires a group choice, who has taken ownership, and what is still waiting on someone or something.

### 6. Money

A shared understanding of expected costs, booked costs, payments, and group expenses, without requiring every trip to become an accounting exercise.

### 7. Trip mode

A calm, time-aware view for the journey itself. It prioritizes today, what happens next, where to go, what to bring or show, and any change that deserves attention.

## Core principles

### Shared truth, personalized attention

There should be one underlying trip, not separate versions for planners and travelers. The app changes what it emphasizes based on what each person needs.

### Reduce the planner's invisible labor

The product should absorb explanation and coordination work, not create another system the planner must constantly maintain for everyone else's benefit.

### Make awareness effortless

A traveler should be able to understand the trip in moments. The product should answer: What is this trip? What has been decided? What changed? What happens next? Is anything expected of me?

### Preserve the difference between possible and decided

Ideas, options, tentative plans, and confirmed bookings must feel meaningfully different. The group should never mistake a suggestion for a commitment.

### Invite contribution without demanding it

People should be able to vote, comment, volunteer, take ownership, or simply follow along. The app should not force equal planning behavior in the name of collaboration.

### Grow calmer as departure approaches

Early planning can be exploratory and expansive. As the trip becomes real, the product should progressively turn decisions into clarity. During travel, it should foreground only what helps the group move confidently through the day.

### Keep the group together without becoming another group chat

Conversation can support the plan, but the plan itself must remain structured, current, and easy to understand. Important information should not disappear into a message history.

## The promise

For the person carrying the trip in their head, Trip Commons provides relief: a trusted place to put it all.

For everyone else, it provides confidence: a simple way to understand the trip and show up as a more informed, helpful traveler.

For the group, it creates alignment: one shared journey, visible to everyone, without requiring everyone to participate in the same way.

## A simple test

Trip Commons is working when:

- The planner no longer has to remember or repeatedly explain everything.
- Every traveler can tell what the trip is and what happens next.
- Open decisions and responsibilities are visible without nagging.
- Confirmed details can be trusted when they are needed.
- The app feels useful both months before departure and in the middle of the journey.

## Not yet decided

This vision intentionally does not choose a platform, business model, technical architecture, or degree of automation. It also does not assume that artificial intelligence is the product. Automation may help collect, organize, summarize, and check the plan, but it should serve the shared understanding at the center of Trip Commons.
